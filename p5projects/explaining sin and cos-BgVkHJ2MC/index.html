Sines, cosines, radians, circles

Draw a line from the center of the circle to its edge at any angle. If the radius of the circle is 1 (the "unit circle"), you can determine the the X coordinate of the end of this line by calculating the cosine of the angle (cos(angle)). Likewise, you can determine the Y coordinate of the end of this line by calculating the sine of the angle (sin(angle)).

In animation and interactive programming, sin() and cos() are good ways to turn a value that linearly increases in value (like frameCount) into a value that oscillates.
